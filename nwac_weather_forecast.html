NWAC Weather Synopsis Issued: 2:33 PM PDT Thursday, April 3, 2025 by Robert Hahn

Weather Synopsis

The Pacific Northwest has entered a quiet weather pattern through Saturday as a ridge of high pressure exerts increasing influence.

Daytime heating has fueled scattered clouds and perhaps an isolated shower. These clouds should dissipate in the early evening hours across the region, yielding mostly clear skies. Meanwhile, a ridge of high pressure expanding inland from the offshore waters will initiate a warming trend, particularly at middle and upper elevations. This will create some inversions overnight as light N winds allow colder air to settle into the valleys. In most cases, below-freezing temperatures are the exception rather than the rule. Light ridgeline winds shift from N to SE.

Temperatures warm significantly on Friday as inversions correct and freezing levels rise to around 7000 ft in the afternoon. Light low-level E flow begins to develop alongside the generally light SE ridgeline winds.

Expect another round of weak inversions Friday night, with even warmer temperatures on Saturday. Mid-level clouds are likely to begin to move into the region by Saturday, so expect a mix of sun and filtered sunshine as freezing levels rise to 9000-10000 ft.
