NWAC Weather Synopsis Issued: 2:38 PM PDT Sunday, April 13, 2025 by Robert Hahn

Weather Synopsis

The high pressure expanding into our region maintains dry weather and clear or mostly sunny skies through Tuesday. Thin high clouds continue to filter over the top of the ridge and will be somewhat more prevalent across NW Washington as the ridge moves to the coast Sunday night and inland on Monday.

Temperatures responded to the incoming ridge on Sunday, but expect further temperature increases Sunday night with 5000 ft low temperatures staying near or above freezing. A few colder snow-covered valleys will have below-freezing temperatures as light winds enable cold air to pool. Monday should be the warmest day of the week, with freezing levels rising to 9500 ft in the north and 11500 ft in the south by the afternoon. Some areas in the southern Cascades could experience some light to moderate SE winds Monday morning as the ridge shifts inland.

Freezing levels remain elevated Monday night as the ridge begins to flatten. A weak disturbance crossing southern British Columbia on Tuesday will likely bring temperatures down a notch as freezing levels lower to 7500-10000 ft.
