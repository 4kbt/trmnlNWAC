NWAC Weather Synopsis Issued: 2:47 PM PDT Monday, April 14, 2025 by Robert Hahn

Weather Synopsis

High pressure will keep the Pacific Northwest warm and dry through the work week.

The ridge flattens Monday night, enabling some increase in upper-level winds that will start to show up as W ridgeline winds gradually increase overnight into Tuesday. By the afternoon, these winds could be moderate at times for areas near and east of the Cascade Crest. Expect a few scattered low clouds in the afternoon alongside some filtered sunshine at times on Tuesday. Ridgeline winds shift NW and increase slightly more as a trough digs down the spine of the Rockies before easing on Wednesday. A few models maintain a slight chance this system touches off a bit of moisture, but most models keep the moisture to our east. Freezing levels peaked in the 9000-11500 range on Monday and gradually start to descend to 8000-10000 ft by Tuesday afternoon and more significantly to 6000-7500 ft by Wednesday afternoon.
